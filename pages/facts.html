Big Bang

The Big Bang, the start of our universe as we know it. The Big Bang is what we call the large explosion that started the still expanding universe that we inhabit. The Big Bang model describes how the universe expanded from a very high-density and high-temperature state, and offers a comprehensive explanation for a broad range of phenomena, including the abundance of light elements, the cosmic microwave background, large scale structures (eg. Nebulas) and Hubble's law which states that galaxies farther away, move faster away from Earth.

Voyager 1

Voyager 1 is a space probe launched by NASA on September 5, 1977. The reason for the Voyager program was to study our solar system. Voyager 1 also has a twin, which was launched 16 days before Voyager 1. After almost 42 years, the probe still communicates with the Deep Space Network to receive routine commands. As of January 1, 2019, it is the most distant human-made object from Earth.

Moon Landing

The moon landing, which it is often called instead of Apollo 11, was the spaceflight that landed the first two people on the Moon. Commander Neil Armstrong and lunar module pilot Buzz Aldrin, both American, landed the Apollo Lunar Module Eagle on July 20, 1969. Armstrong became the first person to step onto the lunar surface six hours after touchdown. After spending 21.5 hours on the lunar surface they rejoined Columbia, piloted by command module pilot Michael Collins, in lunar orbit. This day has become one of the, if not the most important day in all of human space exploration history.

International Space Station

The International Space Station, or ISS as it's often refered to as, is a giant man made low orbit research facility constructed for the purpose of studying the effects of low gravity environments on the human body as well as on plants and other such organic and inorganic matter.

Alpha Centauri

Alpha Centauri is the closest star system and closest planetary system to the Solar System at 4.37 light-years from the Sun. It is a triple star system, consisting of three stars. Inside the trinary star system, we can find some Earth-like planets that resides inside what is known as a Goldilocks zone. Planets inside a Goldilocks zone may harbor intelligent life.
